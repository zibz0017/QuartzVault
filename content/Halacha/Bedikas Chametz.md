# The Issur of Chametz
*This section is extremely important and it needs work*
1. On a dioraysa level, owning chametz on Pesach alone does not violate an issur. The problem is finding this chametz.[^1]
# Ideal Time to Check

1. One should check on the night of the 14th. Chazal were mesaken this because people are home, and the light of a candle shines more clearly at night. This is important for checking in cracks. Once Chazal were mesaken the night of the 14th, that becomes an additional svarah to check on the 14th night. [^2]

# Bedika Before the 14th
1. One can do bedikas chametz at night before the night of the 14th if they are careful not to bring chametz in after they check. There is a chumrah to recheck on the night of the 14th.[^2]
2. One may not check during the day before the 14th night. If one does, they are יוצא בדיעבד and do not need to check again. However, they should recheck one room on the 14th night. Checking by windows, skylights or an אכסדרה (room with only 3 sides[^3] or a room with a lot of windows[^4]) is fine, לכתחילה.[^5] (It seems that one does not make a bracha on this but this is not clear. The Mishna Berura says that if one checks by day in places that are well lit (will be discussed soon) or at night, then one does not make a bracha. But what if one checks in places that are not well lit during the daytime.)
3. Two reasons why we are against doing a bedika by day:
	1. Candles are less effective by day (and sweeping is never particularly effective as it does not reach into cracks.)[^6]
	2. Once the first reason is true, chazal were mesaken it this way and once they are mesaken it this way, we want to stick to it.
4. If one does check before the 14th, they should leave one room unchecked so that on the 14th, they will be able to do a bedika and make a bracha[^7] (Does hiding a few pieces of bread before, as we are noheig, work to allow you to make a bracha on the bedika?)

# Bedika on the 14th Day
1. One should not wait for the 14th day to do the bedika.[^8]
2. If one did wait, then they do not need to use a candle when checking by windows, skylights, or an אכסדרה. For everything else, one should use a candle, just like by night.[^9]
3. They should do the bedika as soon as possible in the morning. They should not eat before doing the bedika.[^8]
# Preparations for Bedika
1. One should sweep their house before doing the bedika.[^10]
2. The minhag is to do this on the 13th day, so that way one is ready to go once the night comes.[^10]
3. There is also a minhag to sweep out cracks using a feather.[^10]
# What Requires Bedika

1. If you are unsure if you brought chametz into a room during the year, you need to do a bedika on that room. If you mistamah did not, then you do not.[^11] (Hard to know what the line is. How unsure do you need to be to say that you need to do a bedika. For example, do you need to clean out a closet? It is unlikely that you brought chametz there. Perhaps chametz was kicked in and in Sif Katan 29 the Mishnah Berurah talks about chametz rolling into a hole, however this svarah is not  mentioned here.)

2. If you have children (perhaps even if you don’t have kids but you had kids over) then you have to check wherever they might have brought chametz, including the bathroom.[^12]

3. Ledges unless they are higher than you can reach or within 3 tefachim of the ground (unless you have kids).[^13]
## Roofs, Porches, and Balconies

1. You do not need to check on something with a sloped roof.[^14]

2. You are required to check on something with a flat roof.[^15] As such, it would seem that one needs to check on a porch.[^16][^17]
## Courtyards

1. The halachos by [[#Animal Cages|animal cages]] apply to courtyards.[^18]

2. In all cases, you have to check the cracks in the walls on the side of a courtyard. [^19][^20]
## Pets

1. If you own a pet, you might not have to do a full bedika. You might only need to sweep out large crumbs from underneath furniture and do a bedika in the kitchen and dining room.[^21]

### Animal Cages

1. If you made sure not to bring Chametz into an animal coop within 30 days of Pesach, then they do not need to be checked.[^22]

2. If there was chametz there within 30 days but before the zman bedika then you should ask a rav.[^22]

3. If there was chametz after the zman bedika, then you actually need to check. You cannot rely on the animal eating the chametz.[^23]

4. You might need to check in cracks in the side of the coop, as is the halacha by [[#Courtyards|courtyards]].
## Bedika on Sefarim

1. The Chazon Ish says that one must do a bedikah in sefarim.[^24]
 
2. Rav Shlomo Zalman Auerbach disagrees because he says that you only need to do a bedika on crumbs bigger than a kezayis. [^25] (Others disagree about crumbs smaller than a kezayis. See [[#^56da67|here]].)

## Crumbs Smaller than a Kezayis

### Do Not Have to do a Bedika

1. Rav Shlomo Zalman Auerbach [[#^8d472e|here]] says that one does not need to do a bedika.

### Have to do a Bedika

1. The Chazon Ish [[#^08bfeb|here]]) would seem to argue as well as the Machane Yisroel [^26] and the Chayei Adam. [^27]
## Holes in the Wall
1. One has to check holes in the wall for chametz as far as one's arm can reach.[^28]
2. If there is chametz beyond one's reach, one does not have to get it. They can rely on the bitul. This is even if there is vaday chametz.[^29]
3. (I don't know if this applies today. It seems that it used to be that people stored chametz in holes in their walls. I don't know if that applies now.)[^16]
## Shuls and Batei Medrashim
1. Shuls and betei medrashi require a bedika.[^30]
2. The shamash should do this on the night of the 14th and make a bracha. However, he can not be mevatel the chametz as it is not his.[^30]
## Purses and Pockets
1. If one puts chametz into their pockets or purse from time to time, they need to do a bedika, even if one is sure that there is not chametz in them.[^31] (I assume that the pockets of clean clothing do not need to be checked. Only the pockets of clothing that one has worn and not yet cleaned.)
2. One should check them during the bedika, and shake them out during the biur because even after the bedika, one may have absentmindedly slipped chametz back into their pocket or purse and not remember it[^32] (I assume that this refers to the pockets on the clothes one wore after the bedika, or a purse only if one used it after the bedika, not every pocket on ones clothes.)
# Getting Rid of Chametz

1. You may throw your chametz into rishus harabim and be mevatel it.[^33]
2. Hiding chametz (like burying it in your rishus) does not help. If the chametz get buried on its own (a wall fell down onto the chametz), it is a machlokes if this is a problem on a dioraysa level.[^1]
