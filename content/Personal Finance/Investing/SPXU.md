# Description
1. SPXU tracks the S&P 500 and is a [[Leveraged ETFs#Reverse Leveraged ETFs|3x reverse ETF]].
# Accurate Tracking
1. Based off of the data in the table, it seems that SPXU largely tracks the S&P 500 accurately (meaning it gives you around a -3x return daily).
2. It is slightly off but as can be seen from the table, the error is slightly in favor of someone shorting the ETF.
# Table

## Explanation of columns
### Column 5
Price change for a theoretical $1000 investment into SPXU. SPXU daily percentage change is divided by -3. We only changed the amount of money on days the market went up. Final values are displayed in bold. (Why all this was done will be explained.)
Because we want to see if SPXU did actually multiply the percentage change of the S&P 500, we have to divide by -3 to compare them.
In addition, if SPXU returns a lower multiple on days the S&P goes up than on days it goes down (ex: on days the market goes up, SPXU only averages a -2.8x return and on days the market goes down it averages a -3.2x return) then that means that the slight variations in the return are working against us shorting SPXU (meaning, if the S&P goes up 1%, we expect SPXU to drop 3%. If it only drops 2.8% and we shorted it, we make less money. And if the market goes down 1%, we expect SPXU to go up 3%. If it goes up 3.2%, then we are losing extra money.
